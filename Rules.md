# Retro Poker 60-2699: Game Rules & Paytable

This game is a 1:1 recreation of the classic RadioShack handheld poker game, following the "Jacks or Better" draw poker variant.

## Gameplay Mechanics
- **Start:** Each player starts a new game with **100 points**.
- **The Ante:** Every round where no points are won, the player **loses 5 points**.
- **Game Over:** If a player's score reaches **0**, it is a "TOTAL GAME OVER," and the game must be reset.

---

## Hand Rankings & Paytable

### 1. Royal Flush — 5,000 Points
The strongest possible hand. It consists of the 10, Jack, Queen, King, and Ace, all of the same suit.
*   **Example:** 10♠, J♠, QS♠, K♠, A♠

### 2. Straight Flush — 250 Points
Any five cards in numerical order of the same suit.
*   **Example:** 5♥, 6♥, 7♥, 8♥, 9♥

### 3. Four of a Kind — 125 Points
Having four cards of the same numerical rank.
*   **Example:** 7♠, 7♣, 7♦, 7♥, 2♣

### 4. Full House — 40 Points
A combination of a "Three of a Kind" and a "Pair" (three of one rank and two of another).
*   **Example:** 10♠, 10♥, 10♦, 4♣, 4♠

### 5. Flush — 25 Points
Any five cards of the same suit, regardless of their numerical order.
*   **Example:** 2♦, 5♦, 8♦, 10♦, K♦

### 6. Straight — 20 Points
Five cards of any suit in a direct numerical sequence.
*   **Note:** This game supports the "Low Ace" straight (A, 2, 3, 4, 5).
*   **Example:** 3♠, 4♥, 5♦, 6♣, 7♠

### 7. Three of a Kind — 15 Points
Three cards of the same numerical rank.
*   **Example:** 9♠, 9♥, 9♦, 2♣, 3♠

### 8. Two Pair — 10 Points
Having two different pairs in the same hand.
*   **Example:** 8♠, 8♥, 4♦, 4♣, 2♠

### 9. One Pair (Jacks or Better) — 5 Points
One pair of cards of the same rank, but **only if the rank is Jack, Queen, King, or Ace**. 
*   **Note:** Pairs of 10s or lower award 0 points.
*   **Example:** J♠, J♥, 4♦, 6♣, 2♠

### 10. GAME OVER — 0 Points
A hand with no matching ranks, sequences, or shared suits.
*   **Result:** Deduction of 5 points from your total score.
